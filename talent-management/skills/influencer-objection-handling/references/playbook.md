# FundLevel Influencer Objection Handling Playbook

## How to Use This

Every objection from a creator is a smokescreen until you find the real reason. The goal is never to accept the no - it's to diagnose what's actually going on so you can address the real problem.

---

## Objection 1: "Not looking for sponsorships right now"

**What they're really saying:** Could be anything. Bandwidth, bad experiences, rates, creative freedom, or just a reflexive no. You don't know yet - and that's the problem.

**How to handle:**

- Acknowledge and respect the no immediately. No pressure.
- Then ask a diagnostic question that fishes out the real reason
- Check Attio for their sponsorship history first
- If they've done sponsorships before, use "going forward" - not "would you ever consider"
- Pick Variant A or B depending on whether you have a relevant brand

### Variant A: You have a desirable brand in their niche

Use this when FundLevel has a brand client that would be attractive to the creator - a brand they'd obviously WANT to work with (not one they've already worked with - there's no pull there).

**The move:** Give them two options where one is obviously more appealing:

> "Is it more of a bandwidth thing right now or are you generally trying to stay working with folks like [aspirational brand] going forward?"

The creator is almost certainly going to say "obviously I want to keep working with cool brands" - and now the conversation is alive. If they object further, they'll reveal the REAL reason: "I want to work with those brands, but I don't like agencies" or "I'm just booked through Q2."

**Example response (DevDuck / Ben - DevTool niche, PostHog is a desirable brand):**
> Hey Ben,
>
> Totally respect that - no pressure at all.
>
> Out of curiosity though, is it more of a bandwidth thing right now or are you generally trying to stay working with folks like PostHog going forward?
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- "Totally respect that - no pressure at all." - Acknowledges the no. Done. Move on.
- "Out of curiosity though" - casual, not salesy
- "bandwidth thing" - gives them an easy, non-confrontational out you can follow up on later
- "folks like PostHog" - social proof. PostHog is a cool brand in DevTools. Every dev creator wants to work with them. Signals FundLevel has real connections.
- "going forward" - implies you already know he's done sponsorships before. You're not asking IF he does sponsorships, you're asking about his direction.
- No unnecessary closing line. No "just helps me know whether to reach out." That adds nothing and plants the idea of being spammed.

### Variant B: You don't have a relevant brand to name-drop

Use this when FundLevel doesn't have a brand client in the creator's niche. Don't bluff - if you don't have a cool brand to dangle, just ask the diagnostic question directly.

**The move:** Same diagnostic question, just without the social proof layer:

> "Is sponsored content no longer a priority for you guys - or is it just a bandwidth thing right now?"

Still diagnoses the real reason. Still keeps the conversation alive. Just doesn't have the brand leverage.

**Example response (Oliur / UltraLinx - premium tech niche, no relevant FundLevel brand):**
> Hey there,
>
> Totally hear you!
>
> Are you able to elaborate on what exactly you guys mean? Is sponsored content no longer a priority for you guys - or is it just a bandwidth thing right now?
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- Still diagnoses: bandwidth vs preference
- "You guys" - addresses management since that's who replied (Oliur's reply came from management, not him directly)
- No fake social proof - doesn't pretend to have a brand in their niche
- Still short, still casual, still keeps the door open

### What to do with their response (both variants):
- If they say "just bandwidth" → follow up in 1-2 months: "Hey, things freed up at all?"
- If they say "I want to work with brands like that but [real objection]" → now address the real objection
- If they say "I'm being more selective about sponsors" → dig into what they look for in a sponsor
- If they say "sponsored content isn't a priority" → that's a real no. Move on.
- If they don't reply → wait 2-3 weeks, follow up once, then move on

**Key principle:** Don't accept the surface-level no. Diagnose the real reason. Use social proof when you have it, go direct when you don't. Never bluff.

## Picking the Right Brand to Name-Drop

The brand you mention should be:
1. **Desirable in the creator's niche** - a brand they'd WANT to work with, not one they already have
2. **A brand FundLevel actually works with** - never name-drop brands you have no relationship with
3. **NOT a brand the creator has already worked with** - there's no pull if they've already done it. The point is to dangle something aspirational.

If you don't meet all three criteria, use Variant B (no name-drop). Don't bluff.

## Universal Principles

- **Every no has a real reason behind it.** Your job is to find it, not accept the surface answer.
- **Ask diagnostic questions.** The goal is to understand the prospect and keep the convo going.
- **Use social proof only when you have it.** Name-drop brands they'd want to work with - not brands they already have. If you don't have a relevant brand, go direct.
- **Never bluff.** If you don't have a desirable brand in their niche, don't pretend you do.
- **"Going forward" over "ever consider."** Signals you know they've done sponsorships before. Check Attio.
- **Cut ruthlessly.** If a line adds no value and makes the email longer, delete it. Creators skim.
- **Never imply you'll spam them.** No "helps me know whether to reach out or leave you be." That's potentially destructive.
- **Shorter is always better.** 2-3 sentences. That's it.
