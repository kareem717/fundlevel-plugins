# FundLevel Influencer Objection Handling Playbook

## How to Use This

Every objection from a creator is a smokescreen until you find the real reason. The goal is never to accept the no - it's to diagnose what's actually going on so you can address the real problem.

---

## Objection 1: "Not looking for sponsorships right now"

**What they're really saying:** Could be anything. Bandwidth, bad experiences, rates, creative freedom, or just a reflexive no. You don't know yet - and that's the problem.

**How to handle:**

- Acknowledge and respect the no immediately. No pressure.
- Then ask a diagnostic question that fishes out the real reason
- The question should be one you somewhat already know the answer to
- Name-drop a brand that would be attractive to them in their niche
- Use "going forward" to signal you know they've done sponsorships before (check Attio first)

**The move:** Give them two options where one is obviously more appealing, so they almost have to engage:

> "Is it more of a bandwidth thing right now or are you generally trying to stay working with folks like [Brand] going forward?"

The creator is almost certainly going to say "obviously I want to keep working with cool brands" - and now the conversation is alive. If they object further, they'll reveal the REAL reason: "I want to work with those brands, but I don't like agencies" or "I'm just booked through Q2" - and now you know what to actually address.

**Example response (DevDuck / Ben - DevTool niche):**
> Hey Ben,
>
> Totally respect that - no pressure at all.
>
> Out of curiosity though, is it more of a bandwidth thing right now or are you generally trying to stay working with folks like PostHog going forward?
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- "Totally respect that - no pressure at all." - Acknowledges the no. Done. Move on.
- "Out of curiosity though" - casual, not salesy
- "bandwidth thing" - gives them an easy, non-confrontational out that you can follow up on later
- "folks like PostHog" - social proof. PostHog is a cool brand in DevTools. Every dev creator wants to work with them. Signals FundLevel has real connections.
- "going forward" - implies you already know he's done sponsorships before. You're not asking IF he does sponsorships, you're asking about his direction.
- No unnecessary closing line. No "just helps me know whether to reach out." That adds nothing and plants the idea of being spammed.

**What to do with their response:**
- If they say "just bandwidth" → follow up in 1-2 months: "Hey, things freed up at all?"
- If they say "I want to work with brands like that but [real objection]" → now address the real objection
- If they say "I'm being more selective about sponsors" → dig into what they look for in a sponsor
- If they don't reply → wait 2-3 weeks, follow up once, then move on

**Key principle:** Don't accept the surface-level no. Ask a question you already know the answer to, load it with social proof, and let them tell you the real reason.

## Picking the Right Brand to Name-Drop

The brand you mention should be:
1. **Cool/desirable in the creator's niche** - PostHog for DevTools, not PostHog for a cooking channel
2. **A brand you actually work with** - don't name-drop brands you have no relationship with
3. **A brand the creator would obviously want to work with** - the question should feel like "do you want free money?" where the answer is obviously yes

## Universal Principles

- **Every no has a real reason behind it.** Your job is to find it, not accept the surface answer.
- **Ask questions you know the answer to.** Stack the deck so the obvious answer keeps the conversation alive.
- **Name-drop strategically.** The brand you mention should be one the creator obviously wants to work with.
- **"Going forward" over "ever consider."** Signals you know they've done sponsorships before. Check Attio.
- **Cut ruthlessly.** If a line adds no value and makes the email longer, delete it. Creators skim.
- **Never imply you'll spam them.** No "helps me know whether to reach out or leave you be." That plants the wrong idea.
- **Shorter is always better.** 2-3 sentences. That's it.
