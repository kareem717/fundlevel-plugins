# Handle: Creator Wants More Context / Feels Lost

**What they're really saying:** "I don't understand what you're offering or who I'd be working with." They're interested enough to reply, but FundLevel's agency model confused them - they expected a brand name and got a middleman. They need clarity before they'll share rates or commit.

This is NOT an objection - it's a fair question. They're engaged. Don't overthink it.

## How to Handle

- Keep it casual and short
- Clarify what FundLevel does without over-explaining
- Move straight toward getting their rates / continuing the conversation
- Pick Handle A or B depending on context

---

## Handle A: Direct Explanation

Use when you don't have specific brands to name-drop, or when the creator just needs a quick "who are you" before sharing rates.

**The move:** TL;DR what FundLevel does, confirm the ask (dedicated video or integration), ask for rates.

**Example (Martin - dev creator who asked "what's your product?"):**

> Hey Martin,
>
> TL;DR: Fundlevel helps brands allocate their influencer marketing spend.
>
> Came across your profile and thought you might make a good fit for a dedicated video or integration in a client's campaign. :)
>
> Let me know if that's something you're comfortable with, and if so - what pricing looks like.

**Why this works:**
- "TL;DR" - signals you're keeping it brief, respects their time
- One sentence explaining FundLevel - no jargon, no over-selling
- Immediately pivots to the ask: are you in, and what's it cost?
- Smiley keeps it casual and friendly
- No unnecessary backstory

---

## Handle B: Name-Drop Potential Clients

Use when you have brands in the pipeline (or brands you're planning to reach out to) that the creator would recognize and want to work with. The brands don't need to be actively in the inbound pipeline - they just need to be real companies you'd realistically pitch.

**The move:** Frame it as "we're onboarding influencers before reaching out to brands like X, Y, Z" - this creates anticipation and positions the creator as someone you're curating for specific opportunities.

**Example (Ivan - dev creator who asked for more context):**

> Hey Ivan appreciate the transparency,
>
> We're currently on boarding a few more influencers before reaching out to brands like Convex, Blacksmith.sh, and PostHog for this campaign. We think an integration with you could potentially be a great fit.
>
> Let me know when you can share the details.
>
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- Name-dropping 3 brands creates weight - feels like a real campaign, not a speculative email
- "Before reaching out to brands" - positions the creator as part of the curation process, not a cold lead
- "We think an integration with you could potentially be a great fit" - specific enough to feel real, vague enough to stay flexible
- "Let me know when you can share the details" - assumes the conversation is moving forward, asks for timing not permission
- No over-explanation of what FundLevel is - the brand names do the talking

---

## When to Use A vs B

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| No relevant brands to name | Handle A |
| Creator is very direct / just wants the basics | Handle A |
| You have 2-3 brands in their niche to name-drop | Handle B |
| Creator seems lukewarm and needs a hook | Handle B |
| Creator is well-known and would attract brands easily | Handle B (shows you have real opportunities) |
