# Brand Reply Handling

Draft replies to brand leads who responded with interest, questions, or engagement - not objections. You're continuing a warm conversation, not overcoming resistance.

## When to Use This vs. Objection Handling

| Signal | Use This Skill | Use Objection Handling |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| Brand asks for creator list / profiles | Yes | No |
| Brand asks about pricing or how it works | Yes | No |
| Brand says "tell me more" or "sounds interesting" | Yes | No |
| Brand asks qualifying questions (budget, format, timeline) | Yes | No |
| Brand says "no budget" / "we're all set" / declines | No | Yes |
| Brand pushes back or goes cold after multiple touches | No | Yes |
| Brand questions the middleman value | No | Yes |

## Core Philosophy

- **Ask about outcomes, not inputs.** Never ask "how many creators?" or "what's your budget?" - that's selling fish at a market. Ask about their dream outcome: what impact they want, what success looks like, what's coming up. Then YOU reverse engineer the inputs (how many creators, what format, what spend) to get them there.
- **Don't pre-load talk tracks.** Ask one question, wait for the answer, then adapt. Your next move depends entirely on what they say.
- **No false promises.** Only commit to what you're ready to deliver right now. "I can totally get that for you" (no timeline) is fine. "I'll send it shortly" is a commitment.
- **Never assume performance.** Even a 500K-view video could mean zero conversions. Ask "did that drive results?" - never say "that must have crushed."
- **Use Attio data before every reply.** Check sponsorship history, company financials, past creator relationships. This data informs your questions and shows the lead you've done your homework.
- **Shorter is always better.** Brand contacts are busy. 2-4 sentences. Every extra line is a reason to deprioritize your email.

## Discovery Talk Tracks

These are different angles for qualifying a lead. Pick the one that fits the situation - they're not steps in a sequence. The goal of all of them is the same: understand what the lead actually wants so you can reverse engineer the right plan. Never ask directly about budget or "how many creators" - that's selling fish at a market.

Read the corresponding talk track file for full details, example questions, and how each track reverse engineers budget:

| Signal | Track | File |
|--------|-------|------|
| Lead mentions goals, KPIs, metrics | Outcome-Focused | `references/talk-tracks/outcome-focused.md` |
| Lead is at a startup, mentions new product/feature, or is in growth mode | Launch / Campaign | `references/talk-tracks/launch-campaign.md` |
| Attio shows past sponsorships you can reference | Expand Past Work | `references/talk-tracks/expand-past-work.md` |
| None of the above / cold start | Outcome-Focused (default) | `references/talk-tracks/outcome-focused.md` |

You can blend tracks. A lead with past sponsorships AND an upcoming launch? Reference the past work (Expand), then ask about the launch (Launch). The tracks are lenses, not scripts.

## When Drafting a Reply

1. **Check Attio first.** Pull their record - sponsorship history, company data, past interactions. This is non-negotiable.
2. **Identify the reply type** from the router above and read the handle file
3. **Match their energy.** If they wrote two sentences, don't reply with five paragraphs.
4. **Ask outcome-focused questions, not input questions.** Don't ask "how many creators" or "what's your budget" - ask "what are you trying to achieve" and "anything launching soon?" Then reverse engineer the plan.
5. **One round of questions at a time.** Pick the 2-3 most important questions for THIS reply. Don't dump a qualification form on them.
6. **Cut ruthlessly.** If a sentence provides no value and makes the email longer, delete it.

## Key Differences from Objection Handling

| | Objection Handling | Reply Handling |
|---|---|---|
| **Their energy** | Resistant or disengaged | Engaged or curious |
| **Your goal** | Keep the door open, diagnose the real reason | Move toward a concrete next step (profiles, call, rates) |
| **Tone** | Disarming, low-pressure | Helpful, peer-to-peer |
| **Questions** | Diagnostic (find the real objection) | Qualifying (understand their needs) |
| **Pacing** | One question per email | 2-3 questions per email is fine |
| **Attio usage** | Verify claims, challenge brush-offs | Inform your questions, reference their history |

## Universal Rules

- **No false promises.** "I can totally get that for you" (no timeline) vs "I'll send it shortly" (commitment). Only the first one is safe unless you're actually about to send it.
- **Never assume performance.** You don't know if their last sponsorship worked. Ask, don't tell.
- **Don't pre-load talk tracks.** One round of questions, wait for the answer, then decide your next move. Different answers lead to completely different follow-ups.
- **Reference, don't interrogate.** "I noticed you guys did a piece with [Creator]" feels like a peer conversation. "What's your budget? What formats do you prefer? What's your timeline?" feels like a form.
- **Use Attio data naturally.** Weave what you know into the conversation - "I saw you ran something with Arjan Codes" - don't present it as research you did on them.
- **Writing style:** No em dashes - use regular dashes or commas. Give paragraphs room to breathe with blank lines between them. Casual tone, lowercase-heavy where natural.
