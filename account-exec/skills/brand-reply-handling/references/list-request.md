# Handle: Brand Asks for Creator List / Profiles

**What they're really saying:** "I'm interested enough to look at what you have, but I'm not committing to anything." This is a good signal - they're engaged. But sending a list without qualifying first is a trap. You'll send the wrong profiles, they'll say "not a fit," and the conversation dies.

## The Problem with Just Sending a List

If you blast over a generic sheet of creators:
- You're guessing at what they want
- They'll judge your entire operation based on profiles that might not be relevant
- You lose the chance to qualify them and understand their setup
- If the list doesn't land, you've burned your shot

The goal is to qualify BEFORE you send - so the list you eventually share actually hits.

## How to Handle

### Step 1: Check Attio First (non-negotiable)

Before you reply, pull their record:
- Have they done sponsorships before? With who? What type?
- What's their company size, ARR, funding?
- Have they worked with creators directly or through agencies?
- Any notes from previous interactions?

This data shapes your entire reply. If you know they ran an integration with Creator X last quarter, you can reference it naturally instead of asking basic questions they'll find annoying.

### Step 2: Qualify Before You Send

Pick 2-3 questions max. Don't dump a qualification form on them. The questions should feel like you're trying to send the RIGHT list, not interrogating them.

**Core qualifying questions (pick 2-3, not all):**

Outcome-focused (preferred - ask about what they want to achieve, then YOU figure out the inputs):
- What's the main thing you're trying to drive - signups, awareness, something else?
- Anything launching on your end that this could tie into?
- When you've run creator content before, what did a good result look like?
- Are you measuring on conversions or more top-of-funnel?

Format/niche (use sparingly - only when you genuinely can't infer from context or Attio data):
- Content-wise - are dedicated videos on the table, or more integrations/mentions?
- Are you focused on a specific market? (NA, EU, global)

Never ask directly:
- ~~How many creators are you looking for?~~ (reverse engineer from their goals)
- ~~What's your budget?~~ (reverse engineer from campaign scale)
- ~~Is there a budget range per creator?~~ (you propose this based on their goals)

### Step 3: Lead with What You Already Know

If Attio shows they've worked with specific creators, reference it. This turns qualification into a peer conversation.

---

## Variant A: You have Attio data on their creator history

Use when Attio shows they've done sponsorships before. Lead with what you know, then qualify.

> Hey [Name],
>
> For sure - I'll put something together.
>
> I saw you guys ran something with [Creator] - are you looking for more of that style, or branching out?
>
> And content-wise - dedicated videos on the table, or more integrations?
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- "For sure - I'll put something together" - no false promise on timing, but confirms you'll do it
- "I saw you guys ran something with [Creator]" - shows you did your homework. Peer conversation, not a cold form.
- Two questions max. Easy to reply to.
- The first question is anchored to something specific (their past work), which makes it feel less like discovery

## Variant B: No Attio data / first interaction

Use when you don't have creator history to reference. Go straight to qualifying questions.

> Hey [Name],
>
> I can totally get that for you.
>
> A couple quick questions so I'm sending relevant profiles and not wasting your time:
>
> Are you guys generally open to flat-fee sponsorships? And content-wise - are dedicated videos on the table, or more integrations/mentions?
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- "I can totally get that for you" - acknowledges the request without a timeline commitment. NOT "I'll send it shortly" (that's a promise).
- "So I'm sending relevant profiles and not wasting your time" - frames qualifying as being respectful of THEIR time
- Two questions. Short. Easy reply.

---

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't just send a generic list.** You'll get "these don't fit" and the conversation dies.
- **Don't say "I'll send it shortly" or "I'll get that over to you."** That's a false promise unless you're literally about to hit send. Use "I can totally get that for you" (no timeline) instead.
- **Don't ask 5+ questions.** Pick the 2-3 most important for THIS lead. You can always ask more in the next reply.
- **Don't assume their last sponsorship went well.** If you reference a past creator relationship, ask "did that drive results?" - don't say "that must have crushed."
- **Don't pre-load your next move.** Their answers determine what you send and how you position it. If they say "integrations only, under $3K" you're sending a completely different list than "dedicated videos, $15K+ budget."

---

## After They Reply to Your Questions

Based on what they tell you, curate a SHORT list (3-5 creators max) that actually matches. When you send it:
- Reference their answers: "Based on what you said about [preference], here are a few that could work"
- Include a mix if possible (different price points, styles)
- Don't oversell any individual creator
- End with a low-friction next step: "Want me to get rates on any of these?" or "Happy to set up a quick call to walk through these if that's easier"

---

## Key Principles

1. **Qualify first, send second.** The list is your proof of value - don't waste it on a blind guess.
2. **No false promises on timing.** "I can totally get that for you" - not "I'll send it shortly."
3. **Lead with what you know.** If Attio has data, use it. It turns discovery into a peer conversation.
4. **2-3 questions max per email.** Don't dump a form on them.
5. **Don't pre-load talk tracks.** Wait for their answers before deciding what to send.
