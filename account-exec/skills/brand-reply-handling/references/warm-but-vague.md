# Handle: Brand Shows Interest but Is Vague

**What they're really saying:** "I'm open to this but I don't know what I want yet" or "I'm interested but not enough to invest real time right now." They replied positively - that's good - but gave you nothing concrete to work with. No budget, no format preference, no timeline. Just vibes.

## The Problem

Vague interest is dangerous because:
- If you treat it as a green light and send a full list, you're guessing
- If you push too hard for specifics, you'll scare off someone who was just casually interested
- If you do nothing, the window closes

The goal is to convert vague interest into ONE concrete next step without making them feel like they're entering a sales funnel.

## Common Vague Replies

- "Sounds interesting, tell me more"
- "Yeah we've been thinking about this"
- "Could be cool, what does it look like?"
- "We're open to it"
- "Let's explore this"
- A short positive reply with no specifics

## How to Handle

### Step 1: Check Attio First

Same as always - pull their record before replying. What you find determines your approach:
- **If they've done sponsorships before:** You can skip the "what is this" explanation and go straight to qualifying. Reference their past work.
- **If they're new to creator marketing:** You might need to briefly explain the model before qualifying.
- **If they have high ARR/funding:** They probably have budget, they just need to be convinced it's worth their time.

### Step 2: Match Their Energy, Then Steer

Don't reply with a wall of text to someone who gave you two sentences. Keep it casual but introduce ONE qualifying question or ONE concrete next step.

---

## Variant A: Outcome-Focused (Default)

Use when: You need more info before you can do anything useful. You don't know their setup and want to understand what they're trying to achieve - not what inputs they want.

> Hey [Name],
>
> Glad to hear it - happy to dig in.
>
> What's the main thing you're trying to drive with creator content - signups, awareness, something else?
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- ONE question about their dream outcome, not about inputs (format, budget, how many creators)
- Their answer tells you everything: "signups" = conversion-focused, dedicated/integration content, measurable. "Awareness" = broader reach, probably mid-to-large creators. "Something else" = they'll tell you exactly what they need.
- You reverse engineer the plan from their answer instead of asking them to design it for you.

## Variant B: Launch / Campaign Angle

Use when: The lead is at a startup or growth-stage company, or you sense something time-sensitive might be happening (new product, feature, expansion). The urgency of a launch naturally reveals scale and budget.

> Hey [Name],
>
> Glad to hear it - happy to dig in.
>
> Are you guys ramping up for anything specific on the marketing side, or more of an always-on play?
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- If they have a launch coming, they'll tell you - and that reveals timeline, urgency, and scale naturally
- "Always-on" tells you they want steady creator content, which means recurring budget
- Either answer gives you a clear next step: launch = "let me put together a campaign plan." Always-on = "let me pull a roster you can rotate through."
- Opens the door to you proposing a full campaign instead of just a list of names.

## Variant C: Lead with Value (Attio shows past creator work)

Use when: Attio shows they've done sponsorships before and you can reference specific creators or campaigns. Leading with what you know skips the vague back-and-forth.

> Hey [Name],
>
> Great to hear. I noticed you guys have worked with [Creator] before - are you looking to expand on that type of content, or going in a different direction?
>
> Happy to pull some profiles based on where you're headed.
>
> Cheers,
> Kareem

**Why this works:**
- References their specific history - immediately shows you're not sending a generic email
- "Expand on that type of content, or going in a different direction?" - gives them two easy options to respond to
- "Happy to pull some profiles based on where you're headed" - positions you as ready to deliver value, not just ask questions
- Doesn't assume their past work went well - just references it as a starting point

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't over-explain FundLevel.** They already replied positively. They get it enough. Don't re-pitch.
- **Don't dump multiple questions.** They gave you a vague reply - one question or one next step. That's it.
- **Don't match vague with vague.** "Yeah for sure, let me know!" leaves the ball in their court and nothing happens. Always steer somewhere specific.
- **Don't assume they're ready to buy.** Vague interest is NOT a commitment. Qualify before you invest real time.
- **Don't send a creator list based on a vague reply.** You don't know what they want yet. Qualify first.

---

## Reading the Vague Reply

Not all vague replies are equal. Try to read what's behind it:

| What They Said | What It Probably Means | Your Move |
|---------------|----------------------|-----------|
| "Sounds interesting, tell me more" | They want to understand the model before committing time | Variant A - ask about their desired outcome |
| "Yeah we've been thinking about this" | They have internal momentum but no clear plan | Variant B - ask about launches/campaigns, or Variant C if you have Attio data |
| "Could be cool, what does it look like?" | They want to see examples before committing | Variant A - understand what they're trying to achieve, then send a curated short list |
| "We're open to it" | Low enthusiasm, testing the waters | Variant A - one outcome question, keep it light |
| "Let's explore this" | Moderate interest, willing to invest some time | Variant B - ask what's on the horizon |

---

## Key Principles

1. **One question or one next step.** Don't overwhelm a vague reply with a wall of options.
2. **Match their energy, then steer.** Casual reply gets a casual response - but always move the conversation somewhere specific.
3. **Don't re-pitch.** They already said yes (loosely). Move forward, don't go backwards.
4. **Use Attio data to skip the basics.** If you know their history, reference it. Skip the "what are you looking for" and go straight to "more of this, or something different?"
5. **Vague interest has a shelf life.** Don't let it sit. Reply within a day while they're still engaged.
