# Talk Track: Expand on Past Work

**Best for:** Leads where Attio shows they've already done sponsorships. You reference what they've done and ask if they want more of it or something different. Their answer reveals direction, and you can naturally ask about what worked/didn't.

## When to Use

- Attio shows past sponsorships you can reference by name
- Your initial outreach already referenced a specific creator collab
- They've been active in the creator space and you want to build on that

## Flow

1. Reference their past creator work (specific creator name, format)
2. More of that, or different direction?
3. What worked, what didn't?
4. You propose the next step based on their answers

## Example Questions

- "I saw you guys did an integration with [Creator] - looking to scale that same format, or branching into something different?"
- "How'd that go for you? Trying to figure out if we should build on that or try a new angle."
- "Are you looking to expand on that type of content, or going in a different direction?"

## How It Reverse Engineers Budget

Their past work IS the budget signal:
- "We want more of the same" → Look up what they paid before (or estimate from creator size). They'll likely spend similar or more per creator.
- "We want to try something different" → They're evolving their strategy, which usually means more budget, not less. Ask about the new direction to size it.
- "That one didn't really work" → They're still talking to you, which means they want to try again with better targeting. Ask what didn't work - the answer tells you what they actually value.

## Blending with Other Tracks

This track blends naturally with the others:
- Past work + outcome-focused: "I saw the [Creator] collab - what were you hoping to get out of that, and did it land?"
- Past work + launch angle: "I saw you worked with [Creator] last quarter - anything new coming up you'd want to build a campaign around?"

## What NOT to Do

- Don't assume the past work went well - always ask, never tell
- Don't just reference the creator name and move on - use it as a doorway into a real question
- Don't say "that must have crushed" or "I bet that drove great results" - you don't know that
