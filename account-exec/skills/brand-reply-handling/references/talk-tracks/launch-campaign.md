# Talk Track: Launch / Campaign Angle

**Best for:** Leads where you sense something time-sensitive is happening - new product, feature launch, rebrand, expansion into a new market. The urgency of a launch naturally reveals scale and budget without asking.

## When to Use

- Lead is at a startup or growth-stage company
- They mention a new product, feature, or expansion
- Attio shows recent funding round (they're spending)
- You sense they're in "build mode" even if they haven't said it explicitly
- Their product/company is clearly iterating fast

## Flow

1. Basic qualification (niche, audience)
2. Any launches or big pushes coming up?
3. Pivot to preparing a full launch campaign or assisting theirs

## Example Questions

- "Anything launching on your end in the next few months that this could tie into?"
- "Are you guys ramping up for something specific, or more of an always-on play?"
- "Is this tied to a product launch, or more general brand awareness?"

## How It Reverse Engineers Budget

Launch urgency does the work for you:
- "We're launching X in 6 weeks" → They need creators NOW, timeline creates urgency, and launches have allocated budget
- "Always-on play" → They want a steady roster, which means recurring monthly spend - you propose the roster size
- "We're expanding into [market]" → Geographic/audience expansion means they need reach, which means multiple creators

The beauty of this track is that launches have deadlines, and deadlines have budgets attached. You don't need to ask - they'll tell you the scope when they describe the launch.

## What NOT to Do

- Don't invent urgency that isn't there - if they say "no rush" believe them and switch tracks
- Don't push for launch details if they're being vague about it - they might not be ready to share
- Don't propose a campaign plan before you understand what they're launching - listen first
