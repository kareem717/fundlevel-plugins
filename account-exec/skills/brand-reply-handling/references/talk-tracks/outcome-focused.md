# Talk Track: Outcome-Focused

**Best for:** Leads who've done sponsorships before, have clear marketing goals, or are at companies with real budget. You're trying to understand what success looks like to them so you can build backward from there.

## When to Use

- Lead mentions goals, KPIs, or metrics
- Company has real budget (high ARR/funding in Attio)
- They've run creator content before and you want to understand what "good" looked like
- Default track when nothing else fits

## Flow

1. Basic qualification (niche, audience type)
2. What does success look like? (signups, awareness, CPM targets, conversions)
3. You propose the plan that gets them there - format, creator count, spend all come from YOU based on their goals

## Example Questions

- "What's the main thing you're trying to drive - signups, awareness, something else?"
- "When you've run creator content before, what did a good result look like for you guys?"
- "Are you measuring this on conversions or more top-of-funnel?"
- "What would a home run look like for you on this?"

## How It Reverse Engineers Budget

Their answer tells you the scale:
- "We want signups" → How many? What's a signup worth to them? That tells you what they'd pay per creator.
- "Awareness in the dev community" → How broad? That tells you how many creators and what size.
- "We're targeting X CPM" → You can back into the right creator mix and total spend from there.

You never ask "what's your budget" - you ask what they want to achieve, then you come back with "here's what it would take to get there."

## What NOT to Do

- Don't ask "how many creators are you looking for?" - that's their answer to give AFTER you propose the plan
- Don't ask "what's your budget range?" - you figure this out from their goals
- Don't rapid-fire multiple outcome questions in one email - pick one, wait for the answer
