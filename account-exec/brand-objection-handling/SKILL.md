# Brand Objection Handling

Draft confident, casual replies to common brand-side sales objections. Every response should keep the door open without being needy. This skill covers objections from brands/prospects - not from influencers/creators.

## Core Philosophy

- Every objection is a smokescreen until proven otherwise
- Goal is never to "overcome" — it's to stay in the conversation
- Always reaffirm first, never argue
- Qualify them too — "whether there's even a fit" takes pressure off

## The 3-Step Handle Framework

Every objection reply should follow this structure:

1. **Acknowledge & Affirm** - "Totally hear ya" / "That makes total sense." Never argue. Show you understand their position.
2. **Relate to Successful Clients** - "Our best clients actually [X]." Reframe the objection as something your best clients have in common, not a dealbreaker.
3. **Actual Handle** - The concrete value-add. This is where you show what FundLevel brings: wholesale pricing, production at scale, access to creators they can't find, managed service, etc.

Then close with a low-friction next step: "Cool if I shoot over a profile here and there?"

## When Drafting a Reply

1. Identify which objection type matches (see `references/playbook.md`)
2. Match the tone of the existing thread — casual if casual, professional if professional
3. Use the handling strategy and example as a starting point, then customize to context
4. Keep it short — 3-5 sentences max for objection replies
5. Never end with the ball in their court — always propose a next step

## Objection Quick Reference

| Objection | Strategy | Go-To Move |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| "What do you do?" | Brief answer, pivot to call | "Easier to nail down in a conversation" |
| "Wrong audience fit" | Acknowledge, pivot to call | "Quicker to hop on a 10 min call" |
| "We're all set" | Respect, ask to stay in touch | "Cool if I shoot over a profile here and there?" |
| "Budget allocated" | Align to their timeline | "Happy to circle back when planning kicks off" |
| "Hired an agency" | Reframe as complementary | "Our most active partnerships are with brands that already have an agency" |
| "Not looking to partner with new agencies" | Clarify you're not pitching AS an agency | "I'm not reaching out as an agency looking to partner" + what you actually do |
| Low budget anchor | Diagnose what's behind the number | "Is that a hard cap, or more about hitting a certain CPM?" |
| "Send proof of work first" | Clarify you're not pitching an agency retainer | "This isn't an agency pitch — happy to do it async" + qualifying Qs |
| "We already work with these creators directly" | 3-step: Acknowledge (you knew), Relate (best clients do too), Handle (pricing/scale/ops) | Show wholesale savings ($5K+), low-cost entry (bigboxSWE $1K post-roll), managed production |
| "We'll reach out" | Take control of follow-up | Don't leave it open-ended, propose a cadence |

For full handling strategies, example responses, and key principles, read `references/playbook.md`.

## Universal Rules

- Name-drop subtly: "One example is bigboxSWE, who we're running with PostHog" — not "We work with PostHog!"
- Calls > emails for anything that takes more than 3 sentences to explain
- "Cool if I shoot over a profile here and there?" is the default keep-warm ask
- "I want to understand if there's even a fit" makes you the selector, not the seller
- Never end with "reach out if anything changes" — always own the follow-up
